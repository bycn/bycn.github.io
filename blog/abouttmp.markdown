i am currently doing a ms at uc berkeley bair advised by Prof Jiao. after my graduation in may '21, i will be joining google search in NYC as a software engineer. 

my research interests are primarily in deep reinforcement learning, where i think about ways to close the gap between theory and practice. i am motivated by the belief that we are still underestimating the capabilities of neural networks + the role of (higher order) tools for productivity. 

previously, i did my bs in computer science at ucb, graduating *summa cum laude* [(don't do this!)](https://cs.stanford.edu/people/karpathy/advice.html). the most influential class for me was ee127 (optimization), which i highly recommend to anyone who is thinking about pursuing ML research. like many, i had a hard time deciding between pursuing industry and research. i did an internship in front-end at glassdoor, where i built the benefits and salary tabs on the [main website](https://www.glassdoor.com/Job/jobs.htm?suggestCount=0&suggestChosen=false&clickSource=searchBtn&typedKeyword=google&locT=C&locId=1147436&jobType=&context=Jobs&sc.keyword=google&dropdown=0). i also did two internships at google writing more traditional software (an anomaly detection system for payments, and visualization pipelines for search). 

on the research side, i pursued undergraduate research advised by Prof Pinto & Sasha Sax, where my [paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.06698) on an experimental study of using pre-trained representations for visual RL was accepted to CoRL '20. i began working with Prof Jiao in my last semester of undergrad as well, briefly pursuing efficient algorithms for robust mean estimation. 

i'm starting a blog because i find a lot of things interesting, especially "lore", that ends up unrecognized due to arbitrary complexity, obscurity, or age. these might be about RL and the future of AGI, but also about my hobbies, learning to learn, mindfulness, navigating cs at berkeley, surprising science (monkeys using currency?) and others... 

in my free time i love watching sitcoms (like bojack & community) and various [movies](https://letterboxd.com/bycn/films/), folding origami, playing [slippi](https://slippi.gg/), doing crosswords, and listen to too much NIKI. Periodically i return to a previous life where i studied violin for many years & was part of the SFSYO.
